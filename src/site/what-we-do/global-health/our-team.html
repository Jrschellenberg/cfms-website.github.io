Our Team

VP Global Health

  • Christoper Briggs
  • University of Manitoba
  • vpglobalhealth@cfms.org
  • Chris Briggs is a third year student from the University of Manitoba. Previously, he completed a Bachelor of Science in Microbiology and Immunology from Dalhousie University. Chris is Manitoba’s CFMS Junior Representative, and also chairs their LGBTTQI* Interest Group. He is currently working on a research degree describing how socioeconomic, behavioural, and demographic factors may impact health outcomes for those living with HIV in Manitoba. 

National Exchange Officers (NEOs)

  • NEO – Outgoing Exchanges
  • Montana Johnston
  • University of Manitoba
  • exchanges.outgoing@gmail.com
  • Montana Johnston is currently in her fourth year of Medical School at the University of Manitoba, and previously completed a three year Bachelor of Science degree in Biology. She has been active within the exchange program for two years as the Local Exchange Officer before taking on the role as the National Exchange Officer last year for CFMS. Montana is a strong advocate for international exchanges and plans to collect meaningful student feedback to dictate future contracts while enhancing CFMS-IFMSA partnerships. 
 
  • NEO – Incoming Exchanges
  • Hillary Pearson
  • University of British Columbia
  • exchanges.incoming@gmail.com
  • Hillary is responsible for incoming exchange students coming to Canada. She works with elective co-ordinators, researchers and local exchange teams at each university to host medical students in clinical clerkships and research internships. She also works with the outgoing exchange officer to grow the CFMS’s exchange program, providing opportunities for Canadian and international students to travel, learn and experience medicine in new cultures.

National Officer of Partnerships (NOP)

  • Michelle Quaye
  • Western University
  • nop@cfms.org
  • Michelle Quaye is a second year medical student at Schulich School of Medicine and Dentistry. She previously completed her Bachelor of Medical Sciences at Western University. Michelle has a strong interest in global health, and is currently Global Health Liaison (GHL) at her medical school. She is also carrying out research focused on global mental health in the Kenyan context, and is part of the inaugural Global MINDS fellowship program at Western University. Michelle aims to improve communication between GHLs across Canada during her time as NOP, and also hopes to continue to improve engagement from Canadian medical students who are interested in global health and social medicine. In her spare time, Michelle enjoys being a Girl Guides of Canada Brownie leader, and travelling to new places.  
 

National Officer of Global Health Education (NOGHE)

 
  • Ruth Habte
  • University of Manitoba
  • noghe@cfms.org
  • Ruth Habte is a third year medical student at the University of Manitoba and has previously completed a Bachelor of Science in Pharmacy. Ruth served as a Global Health Liaison for the U of M during her pre-clerkship years where she facilitated health equity programming, organized the local student-run Global Health Certificate Program and raised funds for local charitable organizations. She is excited to join the national global health program as the incoming NOGHE and is looking forward to applying her passion for health equity, advocacy, and medical education in the portfolio. 

National Officer of Sexual and Reproductive Health (NORSH)

 
  • Benjamin Cassidy
  • Northern Ontario School of Medicine
  • norsh@cfms.org
  • Benjamin Cassidy is a third year student at the Northern Ontario School of Medicine. He has served as LORSH at NOSM over the past year and was the inaugural co-chair of the Canadian Medical Learners for Gender and Sexual Diversity (CML4GSD), a new sub-committee under the NORSH portfolio. He has been asked to speak at a variety of venues in relation to LGBT2SQ+ Health, most recently delivering a talk at the Ontario Medical Student Weekend 2017 on the topic. As NORSH, he would like to continue to build our relationship with IFMSA-SCORA, compile one new and update a few RSH position papers, translate these topics to medical education curricula reform, improve venues for students to contribute to RSH content via a new global health blog, and continue to support and develop the CML4GSD.
