1. Health in Humanitarian Crises free online course

We would like to inform you about the free online course on Health in Humanitarian Crises, developed by the Health in Humanitarian Crises Centre at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine.

This free online course is designed to answer key questions including:

· What are the key health needs in humanitarian crises?

· How can we develop responses to health needs in humanitarian settings with maximum impact?

· How will we respond to health needs in the future?

Find more information about the course and way to apply in the link below.

Link here: https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/health-crises

 

2. Article submissions to the Medical Students International (MSI) #35

First call for article submissions to the Medical Students International (MSI) #35 is now open!

MSI is the IFMSA’s biannual magazine, which focuses on global health perspectives brought by medical students worldwide. Previous issues are available at: http://goo.gl/LpwMxE.

The call will be open for all sections of the magazine:

· March Meeting 2017 Theme "Donate Life: Organ and Tissue Donation" (max 1500 words)

· Six Standing Committee sections (max 750 words)

o Standing Committee on Medical Education (SCOME)

o Standing Committee on Professional Exchange (SCOPE)

o Standing Committee on Public Health (SCOPH)

o Standing Committee on Sexual & Reproductive Health incl. HIV/AIDS (SCORA)

o Standing Committee on Research Exchange (SCORE)

o Standing Committee on human Rights & Peace (SCORP)

When submitting an article, please make sure to review the following checklist:

· Your article in Word or Google docs format. Please do not send in articles in PDF format.

· Images should be of high resolution (at least 300 dpi), in JPEG or PNG formats. Please do not insert images into the article files, but attach them separately to your email

· Author's full name and NMO.

· Author's head shot in high resolution (at least 300 dpi) and in JPEG or PNG formats

· References (if applicable)

Your articles will be reviewed on a rolling basis, so please do not wait for the very last minute to submit them. Deadline for submission: January 3, 2017 @ 23:59 GMT.

All elements must be sent to: msi@ifmsa.org (Please don’t forget to add [MSI35] in the subject of the e-mail)

 

3. Call for Youth PreWHA Participants and World Health Assembly Delegates

We would like to announce the opening of the call for applications for the annual World Health Assembly (WHA) and Youth PreWHA Workshop. The 70th WHA will be held from May 22nd till May 31st, 2017, in Geneva, Switzerland.

WHA is the annual gathering of the highest decision-making body of the World Health Organization. As in previous years, IFMSA will be organizing a Youth PreWHA Workshop on Global Health Diplomacy and Governance â€‹from May 17th till May 21st, 2017, just before the WHA. It is expected that delegates will be able to attend both the PreWHA and the WHA. 

The PreWHA workshop aims to empower and strengthen the advocacy skill set of future health leaders as well as increasing the participants’ knowledge about global health topics that are central to the 70th World Health Assembly.

Please fill out the form in the link below and send your signed and stamped candidature form to prewha@ifmsa.org by January 1st, 2017, 23:59 GMT. Please use the subject of the email “Application PreWHA - Name of Applicant”. Accepted applicants will be notified no later than January 30th, 2017.

You find the application form here: https://goo.gl/forms/9JdIVXtYGRE1c3D63

The registration fee for the Youth PreWHA is TBA (includes food, accommodations) – previously has been between 250 and 500 Euros. 

Participants of the Youth PreWHA Workshop are expected to fully commit to the event’s preparations, contribute to the implementation of the advocacy strategy that will be developed during PreWHA, and sustain involvement during the post-­WHA follow-up. This will include readings, online meetings and preparatory projects. Delegates are expected to commit some time in the leadup to the WHA, including some online meetings between January to May, commit to completing pre-event reading and spend some time preparing statements and organising WHA scheduling pre-event, which may involve a time commitment of up to two hours per week in the leadup to May.
