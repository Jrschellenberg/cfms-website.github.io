1. Blog for Students 4 Best Evidence

Students 4 Best Evidence is a network of students (get inspired by this short video of a medical student) from around the world who are interested in learning more about evidence-based healthcare. Supported by Cochrane UK, this network is looking for students to blog for them. This is a unique opportunity to blog for a world wide network of students, for a website supported by prestigious partners including Cochrane – to promote research for health, inspire students and strengthen sustainable capacities in the Americas.

To become a blogger, access the website Students 4 Best Evidence and complete the application to become a contributor!  Here to sign up for the newsletter.

 

2. 5th International One Health Congress in Saskatoon (June 2018)

The One Health Platform and the University of Saskatchewan are delighted to invite you to join us at the 5th International One Health Congress, which will be held in Saskatoon, Canada, on 22 - 25 June 2018.

We look forward to welcoming you and other One Health advocates to discuss the major global health challenges, including zoonotic diseases such as yellow fever, Zika and West Nile viruses, antimicrobial resistance, and the future challenges of global climate change. More information about the concept, the organizers and the location of the 5th International One Health Congress is available at http://onehealthplatform.com/sites/all/themes/ohpless/img/OHP_5OHC_firstannouncement_v2.pdf

 

3. Barcelona Global Health Summer School 2017 – “Innovation, Access and Health”

We would like to invite you to the Barcelona Global Health Summer School 2017 “Innovation, Access and Health” that will take place from the 16th to the 21st July 2017 in Barcelona, Catalonia – Spain.

The Barcelona Global Health Summer School 2017 will gather key experts in the field to address the causes, contexts, and consequences of the inequitable access to medicines and what to do about it. This course is addressed to students and young professionals studying and working in health-related fields to provide a broad understanding of the complex issue that is access to medicines, from drug development in university labs to prescribing a medicine, exploring the role of different actors, the impact of intellectual property, the need of political will and the genesis of advocacy and activist movements.

Applications meeting the required profile are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis until maximum number of participants is reached. Candidates will be notified of acceptance by email. Interested candidates should apply using the online application form.

You can find all the relevant information of the course in our website, including a tentative program, here: http://bit.ly/BarcelonaGHSS

 

4. Joule Innovation Grants from the Canadian Medical Association

The 2017 round of Joule grants for innovation in medicine is now open!

This year, the CMA is offering grants that target lifecycle stages, including later-stage and early stage ventures. We have also introduced a new social innovation grant; we’ve set aside $25,000 for social initiatives that are focused on improving health care delivery and the health care system—this could include initiatives that reduce health care costs perhaps or maybe decrease wait times.

To apply for a Joule Innovation grant, download the Joule app or visit our website. Applications are due May 1, 2017, 11:59 ET.

Please don’t hesitate to call the CMA Member Service Centre at 1-844-748-8017 if you have questions, or visit cma.ca/innovationgrant
