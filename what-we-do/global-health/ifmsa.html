1. 23rd World Association for Sexual Health Congress – call to join IFMSA delegation 

It’s with great pleasure that I am opening the call for the IFMSA Delegation that will participate in the World Association for Sexual Health (WAS) Congress – an opportunity for medical students to learn about sexology, sexual pleasure and sexual health and rights in academic/scientific setting. Opportunity includes free registration, a workshop on Sexual Health and Sexual Pleasure specifically aimed at medical students, and a spot in a spot in a plenary panel on sexual pleasure.

23rd World Association for Sexual Health Congress

When: May 28 to 31, 2017

Where: Prague (Czech Republic)

What: The World Association for Sexual Health (WAS) is an international umbrella organization representing sexological societies and sexologists worldwide. Founded in 1978 in Rome, Italy, the WAS main goal is to promote sexual health for all through sexological science. They hold biannual congresses, being the upcoming one (23rd) in Prague. More Information: http://www.was2017.org/

General Remarks:

· Delegates are requested to actively participate in the delegation's preparation process for the meeting and contribute to working on IFMSA’s strategy for this meeting.

· Unfortunately, IFMSA won't be able to financially support neither travel nor accommodation for the meeting. We will issue letters of support that will help you in your fundraising efforts, if needed.

Selection Criteria:

· Experience in IFMSA (includes CFMS/local school experiences).

· Experience in the topic of the meeting and work done at local, national and international levels.

· Potential individual empowerment and expected outcomes at a local, national or international level.

· Regional representation and Gender balance will also be taken into account.

Call for input: If you are interested in providing input regarding the activity of IFMSA before and during WAS 2017, please provide it through the input for external meetings form. There will be more information sent via IFMSA communication servers prior to the meeting.

How can you apply?

· Fill in the Application Form

· Candidature Form signed and stamped by the NMO President which needs to be sent to Lra@ifmsa.org with [WAS-APPL] and the name of the applicant in the email subject.

· Both things need to be completed/sent before February 12 23:59 GMT

Questions/concerns? lra@ifmsa.org.

 

2. Blog for Students 4 Best Evidence

Students 4 Best Evidence is a network of students (get inspired by this short video of a medical student) from around the world who are interested in learning more about evidence-based healthcare. Supported by Cochrane UK, this network is looking for students to blog for them. This is a unique opportunity to blog for a world wide network of students, for a website supported by prestigious partners including Cochrane – to promote research for health, inspire students and strengthen sustainable capacities in the Americas.

To become a blogger, access the website Students 4 Best Evidence and complete the application to become a contributor!  Here to sign up for the newsletter.

 

3. 5th International One Health Congress in Saskatoon (June 2018)

The One Health Platform and the University of Saskatchewan are delighted to invite you to join us at the 5th International One Health Congress, which will be held in Saskatoon, Canada, on 22 - 25 June 2018.

We look forward to welcoming you and other One Health advocates to discuss the major global health challenges, including zoonotic diseases such as yellow fever, Zika and West Nile viruses, antimicrobial resistance, and the future challenges of global climate change. More information about the concept, the organizers and the location of the 5th International One Health Congress is available at http://onehealthplatform.com/sites/all/themes/ohpless/img/OHP_5OHC_firstannouncement_v2.pdf

 

4. Webinar: Power and Privilege in the Medical Profession (February 19th, 2017)

The IFMSA has partnered with Women in Global Health to bring you a webinar about "Power and Privilege in the Medical Profession".  This webinar, which will include a panel with 4 great advocates, will explore the intersection of gender, medicine and global health leadership, while providing participants with a platform to discuss the underlying causes that hinder the equal empowerment of genders. The webinar will take place February 19 at 8pm GMT. More information on the speakers as well as the event link will soon be released in the different communication channels, so stay tuned!

 

5. Training Disaster Medicine Trainers 2017 course (Deadline: February 19, 2017)

Apply for the international Training Disaster Medicine Trainers 2017! It begins with three months online learning of roughly 2 hours time commitment per week (April 1 to June 30, 2017), followed by one week residential course in Novara, Italy (July 17 to 23, 2017). Registration cost is 450 Euros.

The program consists of classes with both conventional and innovative teaching strategies, computer based and real life simulations. Part of the residential course will take place in tents the Red Cross Italy uses in disaster response. Students will stay in these tents for three nights, also using them for simulated disaster response activities. The students will learn from experts of the emergency and disaster medicine community. At the end of the program, you will possess extensive knowledge about disaster medicine and become a certified IFMSA Disaster Medicine Trainer. This will give you the skills and knowledge to deliver disaster medicine courses to other medical students within your communities, IFMSA General Assemblies and Regional Meetings. TdmT graduates are part of a global network of medical students with the skills and knowledge of delivering a well-planned training course on disaster medicine.
