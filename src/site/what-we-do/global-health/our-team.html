Our Team

VP Global Health

  • Christoper Briggs
  • University of Manitoba
  • vpglobalhealth@cfms.org
  • Michelle Quaye is a third year medical student at Schulich School of Medicine and Dentistry. She previously completed her Bachelor of Medical Sciences at Western University. Michelle has a strong interest in global health, and is currently Global Health Liaison (GHL) at her medical school. She is also carrying out research focused on global mental health in the Kenyan context, and is part of the inaugural Global MINDS fellowship program at Western University. Michelle aims to improve communication between GHLs across Canada during her time as NOP, and also hopes to continue to improve engagement from Canadian medical students who are interested in global health and social medicine. In her spare time, Michelle enjoys being a Girl Guides of Canada Brownie leader, and travelling to new places.  

National Exchange Officers (NEOs)

  • NEO – Outgoing Exchanges
  • Montana Johnston
  • University of Manitoba
  • exchanges.outgoing@gmail.com
  • Hillary is responsible for incoming exchange students coming to Canada. She works with elective co-ordinators, researchers and local exchange teams at each university to host medical students in clinical clerkships and research internships. She also works with the outgoing exchange officer to grow the CFMS’s exchange program, providing opportunities for Canadian and international students to travel, learn and experience medicine in new cultures. 
  • NEO – Incoming Exchanges
  • Hillary Pearson
  • University of British Columbia
  • exchanges.incoming@gmail.com

  • Charly Coleman is excited to join the Global Health team at the CFMS.  She is a second-year medical student at Queen’s University. Working with the National Exchange Officer Incoming, the local exchange officers at Canadian universities, and other National Officers abroad, she wants to continue to provide meaningful and ethical experiences for Canadian students to undergo international learning and research opportunities.

National Officer of Human Rights and Peace (NORP)

  • Asha Behdinan
  • University of Toronto
  • norp@cfms.org

  • Asha Behdinan is a third year medical student at the University of Toronto. She previously completed her Bachelor of Arts and Science at McMaster University with a specialization in economics. Over her preclerkship years, she has been involved in the Day of Action research committee and has continued to work on opioid overuse research on behalf of CFMS. At U of T she has also led initiatives on antimicrobial resistance and social justice in medical education. This year, Asha aims to increase the CFMS involvement within the International Federation of Medical Students Associations, as well as launch a national advocacy campaign on the annual theme of refugee and migrant health. She is very excited to bring her passion and enthusiasm for global health advocacy to the NORP portfolio this year.

National Officer of Global Health Education (NOGHE)

  • Ruth Habte
  • University of Manitoba
  • noghe@cfms.org

National Officer of Sexual and Reproductive Health (NORSH)

  • Benjamin Cassidy
  • Northern Ontario School of Medicine
  • norsh@cfms.org
  • Benjamin Cassidy is a third year student at the Northern Ontario School of Medicine. He has served as LORSH at NOSM over the past year and was the inaugural co-chair of the Canadian Medical Learners for Gender and Sexual Diversity (CML4GSD), a new sub-committee under the NORSH portfolio. He has been asked to speak at a variety of venues in relation to LGBT2SQ+ Health, most recently delivering a talk at the Ontario Medical Student Weekend 2017 on the topic. As NORSH, he would like to continue to build our relationship with IFMSA-SCORA, compile one new and update a few RSH position papers, translate these topics to medical education curricula reform, improve venues for students to contribute to RSH content via a new global health blog, and continue to support and develop the CML4GSD.
